If you manage a rental in Vista, drywall damage shows up fast between tenants. Small anchor holes, door knob dents, torn paper, and larger patches from shelving or TV brackets are common. The question is not whether you will have to repair drywall. It is whether the repair will hold up, match well enough, and be ready before the next move-in.

In Vista, that can depend on the age of the property. Older homes may have heavier texture or uneven past repairs. Newer units often have more uniform finishes, which means a bad patch stands out even more.

If you are already planning turnover work, start with the [Vista service area page](/service-areas/vista/) so you can group repairs efficiently.

## What to check during turnover

Before you decide whether to patch it yourself or schedule help, walk the unit and look for patterns.

### Common drywall issues in Vista rentals

- **Nail and anchor holes** from wall decor
- **Medium wall gouges** from furniture moves
- **Torn drywall paper** after hardware removal
- **Corner bead damage** near hallways
- **Texture mismatches** from old patch jobs

If damage is limited to a few clean holes, you may be able to handle it. If the wall has multiple repair spots, visible texture, or larger blown-out sections, it usually makes more sense to schedule [drywall repair service](/services/drywall-repair/).

## When a DIY patch is reasonable

DIY works best when the job is small and the finish does not need to be exact.

A basic patch may be reasonable if:

1. The holes are **small and shallow**
2. The paper face is **not badly torn**
3. The wall has **minimal texture**
4. You have enough time for **drying, sanding, and repainting**

That last point matters. Many turnover repairs fail because the patch is rushed. Spackle shrinks, sanding gets skipped, and the paint flashes in afternoon light. In Vista rentals, especially in units with strong window light, that shows immediately during walkthroughs.

## When to schedule the repair instead

There are a few cases where DIY usually costs more time than it saves.

### Schedule service when:

- The wall has **orange peel or knockdown texture**
- You need **multiple repairs in different rooms**
- There are **larger holes or loose drywall**
- The patch area will be in a **high-visibility space**
- Move-in timing is tight

Texture matching is where most rental owners get stuck. A flat patch on a textured wall is obvious from across the room. Even if the wall color is close, the finish will not blend right.

For bundled turnover work, it may also help to look at the [small repairs and honey-do list service](/services/honey-do-lists-small-repairs/). That is useful when you have drywall issues plus loose hardware, damaged trim, or minor punch-list items.

## A practical turnover checklist

- **Mark every damaged wall area before paint**
- **Separate small holes from larger patch work**
- **Check corners, stairwells, and behind doors**
- **Look for old repair spots that need rework**
- **Plan enough time for patching, drying, sanding, and paint**
- **Do not leave texture decisions until the last day**

## How to avoid delays before the next tenant

The best time to handle drywall is before cleaning and final touch-up painting. Once the unit is staged for turnover photos or walkthroughs, patch dust and repainting become a bigger hassle.

For faster scheduling, use the [contact page](/contact/) and note the number of repair areas, whether texture matching is needed, and your target ready date.

Between tenants, drywall work should be simple, clean, and timed right. If the repair is small, DIY may be enough. If it needs texture matching or multiple patches, scheduling the work early usually saves you from last-minute problems.
