## Common Electrical Issues in North County Oceanside Homes

### Outdated Wiring & Panel Upgrades

A lot of Oceanside homes built in the 1970s and 1980s still run on 100-amp service. Modern homes need 150 or 200 amps, especially if you're adding air conditioning, electric vehicle charging, or running multiple appliances. We assess your panel, check your usage, and tell you whether an upgrade makes sense or if you can get by with what you have.

### Salt Corrosion & Outdoor Fixtures

If you live near the coast—whether Oceanside proper or just north in Carlsbad—salt spray corrodes electrical connections, outdoor outlets, and light fixtures faster than inland. We replace corroded outlets, install weatherproof covers, and use marine-grade materials where it matters. That extra step keeps your system safe and adds years to outdoor lighting and outlets.

### Flickering Lights & Loose Connections

Flickering lights usually mean a loose connection somewhere—at the outlet, breaker, or even the meter. It's not always obvious, but it's a fire hazard if left alone. We trace the circuit, tighten connections, and make sure everything is secure.

### Tripped Breakers & Circuit Overload

One breaker keeps tripping? Could be an overloaded circuit, a faulty breaker, or a short in the wiring. We identify what's causing it and either redistribute the load, replace the breaker, or add a new circuit if you genuinely need more capacity.
