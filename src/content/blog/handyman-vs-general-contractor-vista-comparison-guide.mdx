You've got a leaky faucet, a wobbly deck, or maybe drywall that needs patching. Or you're thinking about a full kitchen remodel. Either way, you're wondering: do I call a handyman or a general contractor?

The answer matters. Hiring the wrong person costs you time and money. Hiring the right one gets the job done right, on budget, and without unnecessary overhead.

Here's the straight answer for Vista homeowners: **a handyman is the right choice for most small-to-medium repairs and projects under $5,000. A general contractor is what you need for major renovations, structural work, or anything requiring permits.**

Let's break down the real differences so you can decide with confidence.

## Scope of Work: What Each Can Actually Handle

A handyman handles the jobs that don't require a contractor's license or permit. This includes:

- Furniture assembly and installation
- TV mounting
- Minor drywall repair
- Replacing fixtures (faucets, outlets, light switches)
- Carpentry and woodwork (shelving, trim, deck repairs)
- General honey-do list items

A general contractor handles larger, more complex projects—often ones that touch multiple trades. This includes:

- Full room remodels
- Structural changes
- Major electrical rewiring
- Plumbing system overhauls
- Building additions
- Any work requiring permits and inspections

The key distinction: if your project requires a permit from Vista's building department, you need a licensed general contractor. If it doesn't, a handyman can do it faster and cheaper.

## Licensing and Insurance: Why It Matters

In California, general contractors must be licensed by the Contractors State License Board (CSLB). They carry bonding and insurance that protects you if something goes wrong.

Handymen don't need a contractor's license for small repairs. But a reputable handyman carries liability insurance and workers' comp. Always verify this before hiring.

For Vista residents: if your project involves structural work, electrical systems, or plumbing that ties into the main line, the city requires a licensed contractor. Don't try to save money here—it creates liability for you and violates code.

## Cost: The Real Breakdown

**Handyman pricing:**
- Hourly rates: $50–$100
- Flat rates for specific jobs: $150–$500
- Most projects: under $2,000

**General contractor pricing:**
- Hourly rates: $75–$150+ (plus crew costs)
- Project markup: 15–20% of total cost
- Most projects: $5,000–$50,000+

A handyman doesn't carry the same overhead as a contractor. They're usually solo or a small team. That means lower costs for you.

But here's the catch: if a general contractor takes on a small job, they'll still charge a minimum (often $500–$1,000) because of their business model. It's not worth their time otherwise.

For a $300 drywall patch in Vista, call a handyman. For a $40,000 kitchen remodel, call a general contractor—they have the crew, project management, and warranty backing to deliver.

## Timeline and Availability

Handymen typically start faster. Many can fit you in within a few days because they're managing their own schedule and don't have a backlog of large projects.

General contractors manage multiple crews and projects. You might wait 2–4 weeks for a start date, especially during peak season (spring through fall in San Diego County).

For urgent repairs—a burst pipe, broken door frame, or failed electrical outlet—a handyman gets you fixed quickly. For planned renovations, the wait for a contractor is normal and expected.

## Warranty and Accountability

This is where contractor licensing matters. A licensed general contractor carries surety bonds and is accountable to the state. If they don't finish the job or do poor work, you have recourse through the CSLB.

A handyman operates on reputation and word-of-mouth. That's usually fine—many handymen are meticulous and stand behind their work. But your legal protection is thinner. Always get a written agreement, even for small jobs.

## When to Choose a Handyman

Pick a handyman if:

- Your project is under $5,000
- It doesn't require a permit
- It's a single-trade job (carpentry, plumbing fixtures, electrical outlet replacement)
- You need it done quickly
- You want to avoid contractor overhead

Examples: [furniture assembly](/services/furniture-assembly-installation/), [TV mounting](/services/tv-mounting/), fixture replacement, shelving installation, minor [drywall repair](/services/drywall-repair/), deck staining.

## When to Choose a General Contractor

Pick a general contractor if:

- Your project exceeds $5,000
- It requires a permit from Vista
- It involves multiple trades (electrical, plumbing, framing, drywall, finishing)
- It's structural or load-bearing
- You need project management and a crew
- You want a warranty and CSLB accountability

Examples: kitchen or bathroom remodels, room additions, major electrical rewiring, foundation repair, full renovations.

## The Recommendation for Vista Homeowners

**For most repairs and smaller projects, hire a handyman.** You'll save money, get faster service, and avoid paying for contractor overhead on work that doesn't need it.

For major renovations or anything requiring permits, hire a licensed general contractor. The cost difference reflects real value—project management, crew coordination, permits, inspections, and warranty backing.

The mistake most homeowners make is calling a general contractor for a $300 job or hiring an unlicensed handyman for permitted work. Both cost you money or create problems.
