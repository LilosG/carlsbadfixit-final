Summer in Carlsbad moves fast. Coastal air, increased foot traffic, and the shift to outdoor living expose all the small repairs that got pushed through winter. A focused pre-summer visit works best when the list is grouped by area and realistic for one or two scheduled visits.

Use this checklist to build a clear plan before the season picks up.

## How to use this checklist

- Start with anything that affects comfort or function daily.
- Group exterior and interior repairs into separate visits if the list is long.
- Flag anything that needs a material order so it does not slow down the visit.

## Carlsbad pre-summer maintenance checklist (15 repairs)

**Exterior and entry**

1. Inspect and tighten gate hinges, latches, and fence hardware loosened by coastal air.
2. Replace worn weatherstripping at the front door and any sliding doors.
3. Check wood trim, fascia, and window frames for soft spots from winter moisture.
4. Re-caulk exterior gaps at window frames, door casings, and utility penetrations.
5. Secure any loose deck boards, steps, or handrail hardware before outdoor use picks up.

**Plumbing fixtures**

6. Check outdoor hose bibs for drips after winter pressure changes.
7. Replace any bathroom faucet aerators with mineral buildup from hard water.
8. Test garbage disposal for noise or slow draining before summer cooking volume increases.

**Electrical and lighting**

9. Replace burnt-out exterior lights and any flickering bulbs before summer evenings.
10. Test GFCI outlets near water — kitchen, bathrooms, and any outdoor outlets.
11. Check ceiling fan direction and balance before hot weather arrives.

**Interior quick fixes**

12. Adjust sticky doors that swelled through winter humidity changes.
13. Patch small wall dings, nail holes, and drywall cracks before any interior touch-up painting.
14. Re-secure loose towel bars, robe hooks, and bathroom accessories.
15. Tighten loose cabinet hardware throughout the kitchen and bathrooms.

## What fits into one visit?

Most Carlsbad homeowners can cover the full exterior and entry section plus three or four interior fixes in a single focused visit. ## Local tips for Carlsbad homes

- Salt air accelerates corrosion on exterior hardware faster than inland properties. Gate hinges, door handles, and fence hardware are the first places to check every spring.
- Homes in La Costa, Aviara, and Bressi Ranch often have stucco exteriors — re-caulking window and door casings is a high-value annual maintenance step.
- Carlsbad builder-grade plumbing fixtures from the 1990s and early 2000s are reaching the end of their service life. Pre-summer is a practical time to replace rather than repair.
